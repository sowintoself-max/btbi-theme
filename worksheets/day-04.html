Brand Positioning & Business Identity.

The Strategic Decision Behind Your Business Name.

Why This Is a CEO Decision

Day 3 gave your business a vision. Today, we look at how that vision will be perceived. Your business name is more than a title — it is your first brand impression, your legal identity, and the foundation of everything you will build. But the name itself is only the output of something larger: your brand positioning. Even if you have already chosen a name, today's work is not wasted. The discipline of evaluating your name against your vision is the work of a CEO — and it does not stop after your business is named.

§ IVThe Five CEO Questions

Test your top name choice against the five questions a CEO asks before committing.

Top name choice for CEO evaluation:
CEO QuestionYesNoNotes
1. Does this name align with my Day 3 vision?
2. Will this name still serve me when my business doubles?
3. Does this name position me for the clients I want tomorrow — not just today?
4. Will I be proud to say this name out loud, in any room, to any client?
5. Does this name leave room to grow — or lock me into something narrower than my future?

If your top choice passes all five questions, you have your name. If you are an established business whose existing name passes all five, today is the day to recommit to it with new clarity.

§ VIIYour Daily Reminder

Once you have committed to your business name, write it by hand on a card. Print it. Place it somewhere you see every day — your office, your home, your workspace. Let it become a daily reminder that this business is no longer a maybe. It is named, it is yours, and it is being built — on purpose.

"The best business names are not clever. They are aligned."
