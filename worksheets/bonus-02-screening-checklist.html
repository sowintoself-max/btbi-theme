The Foundational Principle

CEOs do not screen clients to be exclusive. They screen clients to be intentional.

Every yes you give is a no to something else — your capacity, your other clients, your boundaries, and the higher-aligned client who could have taken that spot. The CEO Client Screening Checklist™ is how you make sure each yes is the right one. The goal is not to disqualify everyone — it is to ensure that every client you accept supports your time, your brand standards, and your long-term profitability.

§ IVDecision-Making Behavior

This is the filter most travel professionals underestimate — and the one that costs them the most hours.

§ VRisk & Responsibility Awareness

A client who understands that travel includes inherent risk treats your guidance as expertise — not as a guarantee.

§ VIThe CEO Quick-Evaluation

After your initial consultation — before you send any quote, agreement, or deposit invoice — pause and answer these three questions honestly.

QuestionYesNo
Does this client respect my process?
Does this client fit my niche and brand?
Does this client support my income goals without burnout?

💡If you answer No to more than one, consider declining or referring out.

This is the discernment that protects your business. Not every "no" needs explanation. Not every client needs your service. The right ones will find you. Your job is to make sure you are still available when they do.

§ VIIYour CEO Boundary Statement

Use this language internally — or directly with clients when needed:

"To protect the quality of service I provide, I only work with clients who align with my process, communication standards, and planning expectations."

Standards do not need defending. They simply need to be maintained.

A Final Note from Bobbie

Many travel professionals are afraid that screening clients will cost them business. The opposite happens.

When you maintain clear standards, three things happen at once: the right clients trust you faster (because professionalism signals expertise), the wrong clients self-select out (saving you time you would have wasted), and your business stops attracting what it has been tolerating (because clarity is its own form of marketing).

Standards are not what limit your growth. Standards are what create the conditions for the growth you have been working toward.
